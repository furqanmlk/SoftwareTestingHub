# AI Automation Architect - Advanced Interview Preparation

## Section 1: AI Testing Strategy

### Q1. Why do organizations need AI-driven testing?
**Answer:** AI reduces manual effort, accelerates releases, improves defect detection, and optimizes test execution.

**Implementation:**
- AI test generation
- Failure analysis
- Intelligent test selection
- Self-healing automation

---

### Q2. What are the biggest challenges in AI testing?
**Answer:** Non-deterministic outputs, hallucinations, data quality, model drift, and lack of traditional assertions.

**Implementation:**
- Semantic validation
- Benchmark datasets
- LLM-as-a-Judge
- Human review process

---

### Q3. How would you create an AI testing roadmap?
**Answer:** Assess → Standardize → Automate → Introduce AI → Scale enterprise-wide.

---

## Section 2: Agentic AI

### Q4. What is an AI Agent?
**Answer:** An autonomous system that can reason, plan, act, and evaluate outcomes.

### Q5. How would you test an AI Agent?
**Answer:** Validate goal completion, reasoning quality, tool selection, and recovery behavior.

**Implementation:**
- Test objectives
- Tool invocation validation
- Result validation
- Error handling validation

---

### Q6. Multi-Agent Testing?
**Answer:** Verify agent collaboration, communication, conflict handling, and final outcomes.

## Section 7: Tool Comparisons

### Q20. Playwright vs Selenium?
**Answer:**

- Built-in waits

- Enterprise adoption

---

### Q21. Mabl vs Testim vs Functionize?
**Answer:**

Mabl:
- Low-code
- AI focused

Testim:
- Strong self-healing

Functionize:
- NLP-driven automation

Selection depends on business needs.

AI tools complement automation frameworks but do not replace engineering control, scalability, or customization.
